PAKISTAN PENAL CODE (PPC), 1860 - SUMMARY OF CORE SECTIONS

Section 300: Qatl-i- عمد (Murder)
Whoever, with the intention of causing death or bodily injury likely to cause death, causes death is said to commit murder.
Punishment (Section 302): Death, imprisonment for life, or Qisas.

Section 375: Rape
Definition includes sexual intercourse against will, without consent, or with consent obtained by fear of death/hurt.
Punishment (Section 376): Death or imprisonment for 10-25 years and fine.

Section 405: Criminal Breach of Trust
Dishonestly misappropriating or converting property entrusted to one's care.
Punishment: Imprisonment up to 7 years or fine or both.

Section 420: Cheating and Dishonestly Inducing Delivery of Property
Cheating and thereby dishonestly inducing the person deceived to deliver any property.
Punishment: Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.

CODE OF CRIMINAL PROCEDURE (CrPC), 1898 - KEY PROCEDURES

Section 154: First Information Report (FIR)
Information relating to a cognizable offence given to police must be recorded in writing and signed by the informant.

Section 496: Bail in Bailable Cases
In bailable offences, the accused has a right to be released on bail if they are prepared to provide it.

Section 497: Bail in Non-Bailable Cases
Bail may be granted at the court's discretion, except for offences punishable by death or life imprisonment.

PREVENTION OF ELECTRONIC CRIMES ACT (PECA), 2016 - CORE OFFENSES

Section 3: Unauthorized access to information system or data.
Punishment: Up to 3 months imprisonment or 50,000 PKR fine.

Section 4: Unauthorized copying or transmission of data.
Punishment: Up to 6 months imprisonment or 100,000 PKR fine.

Section 10: Cyber-terrorism.
Punishment: Up to 14 years imprisonment and 50 million PKR fine.

Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment or 1 million PKR fine.

Punishment: Up to 5 years imprisonment or 5 million PKR fine.

Section 24: Cyber-stalking.
Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment or 1 million PKR fine.

MUSLIM FAMILY LAWS ORDINANCE, 1961 - KEY PROVISIONS

Section 5: Registration of Marriages
Every marriage solemnized under Muslim Law shall be registered with the Union Council. Nikah Registrars are appointed for this purpose.

Section 6: Polygamy
No man, during the subsistence of an existing marriage, shall contract another marriage except with the previous permission in writing of the Arbitration Council.

Section 7: Talaq (Divorce)
A husband wishing to divorce must give notice in writing to the Chairman of the Union Council. Divorce is not effective until 90 days after the notice is delivered.

Section 9: Maintenance
If a husband fails to maintain his wife adequately, she may apply to the Chairman for a maintenance certificate via an Arbitration Council.

FIA COMPLAINT PROCEDURES (SUMMARY)

1. Online Reporting: Visit complaint.fia.gov.pk or nr3c.gov.pk.
2. In-Person: Visit any Cyber Crime Reporting Center (CCRC) in major cities (Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi, etc.).
3. Requirements: Written complaint, CNIC copy, and evidence (screenshots, links, bank statements).
4. Reference Number: Always obtain and save the complaint reference number for tracking.

CONSUMER PROTECTION RIGHTS (PUNJAB & SINDH)

1. Right to Refund/Replacement: If you buy a defective product or receive poor service, you have a legal right to get it fixed, replaced, or refunded.
2. 15-Day Notice Rule: Before going to court, you MUST send a written notice (via TCS or registered post) to the seller. Give them 15 days to resolve the issue. Keep the receipt!
3. Consumer Court: If they don't respond in 15 days, you can file a case in the Consumer Court. There is NO fee to file a case here, and you don't always need a lawyer for small claims.
4. Price Display: Shops are legally required to display prices clearly. Overcharging is a crime.

---

RENT AND TENANCY LAWS (PUNJAB RENTED PREMISES ACT)

1. Written Agreement: Every tenancy MUST be in writing and registered. An oral agreement is hard to prove in court.
2. Rent Increase: Rent usually increases by 10% per year unless a different amount is written in the agreement.
3. Eviction Rules: A landlord cannot kick you out without a reason. Valid reasons include: not paying rent for 2 months, using the house for a purpose not in the contract, or if the landlord needs it for their own family (with notice).
4. Utilities: A landlord cannot cut off your electricity, water, or gas to force you to leave. This is illegal.
5. Maintenance: The landlord is responsible for major repairs (structural), while the tenant handles minor everyday maintenance.

---

LABOR AND WORKER RIGHTS

1. Minimum Wage: As of 2024-2025, the minimum wage for an unskilled worker is approx. 37,000 to 40,000 PKR depending on the province.
2. Appointment Letter: Always demand an appointment letter. If you work for 9 months in the same position, you are legally considered a "Permanent Employee."
3. Termination Notice: A permanent employee must be given 30 days' notice before being fired, or 30 days' extra salary.
4. Working Hours: Standard work is 48 hours a week (9 hours a day max). Anything more must be paid as "Double Overtime."
5. Maternity Leave: Women are entitled to 12 weeks of paid leave for childbirth.

---

FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS (CONSTITUTION OF PAKISTAN)

1. Equality: Article 25 says all citizens are equal and there shall be no discrimination on the basis of sex.
2. Education: Article 25-A says the State must provide free and compulsory education to all children aged 5 to 16.
3. Fair Trial: Article 10-A guarantees the right to a fair trial. You cannot be punished without being heard.
4. Speech & Religion: Every citizen has the right to express their opinion and practice their religion freely (within the law).
5. Protection from Arrest: If arrested, you must be told why, and you must be presented before a judge (Magistrate) within 24 hours.

---

PROTECTION OF WOMEN AGAINST VIOLENCE (PUNJAB ACT)

1. Domestic Violence: Abuse (physical, emotional, or economic) is a crime. It is not a "private family matter."
2. VAW Centers: Specialized Violence Against Women Centers provide police, medical, and legal help under one roof.
3. Right to Stay: A woman cannot be forcefully evicted from her house by her husband or relatives. The court can grant a "Residence Order."
4. Helpline: Call 1043 (in Punjab) for immediate help with women's protection issues.
